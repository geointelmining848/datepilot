# Weekly Review

Run this lightly, not obsessively. The goal is to distill signal, not manufacture process.

## When to Run

Use during a heartbeat or whenever enough work has accumulated. Rough target: once every few days to once a week.

## Inputs

Read only what is needed:

- recent `memory/YYYY-MM-DD.md` files
- `MEMORY.md`
- `.learnings/LEARNINGS.md`
- `.learnings/ERRORS.md`
- `.learnings/FEATURE_REQUESTS.md`

## Review Questions

1. What important facts or preferences appeared repeatedly?
2. What mistake or confusion happened more than once?
3. What workflow rule would have prevented avoidable friction?
4. What should stay as raw history and not be promoted?
5. What is stale and should be ignored or pruned?

## Output

Use this compact format:

```markdown
WEEKLY REVIEW
- Repeated facts:
- Repeated lessons:
- Candidate promotions:
- Candidate workflow upgrades:
- Candidate tool notes:
- Candidate skills/checklists:
- Items to prune or ignore:
- Net expected benefit:
```

## Promotion Limits

Default limits per review:

- at most 3 promotions into `MEMORY.md`
- at most 2 workflow updates into `AGENTS.md` or `TOOLS.md`
- at most 1 new skill/checklist draft

If there is more than that, batch the remainder in `.learnings/` and revisit later.

## Pruning Rule

If an old item has not influenced behavior and no longer seems likely to matter, do not promote it. Shorten or ignore it.

## App Business Bias

Prefer promoting anything that improves:

- app idea screening
- monetization judgment
- App Store execution
- shipping speed
- debugging discipline

Prefer not promoting generic philosophy unless it reliably changes action.
