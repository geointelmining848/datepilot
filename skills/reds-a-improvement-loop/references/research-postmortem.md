# Research Postmortem

Use this after rejecting an app idea, failing to validate one, or discovering that an apparent opportunity is weaker than expected.

## Purpose

Prevent repeated bad bets. Distill why an idea looked good, why it failed scrutiny, and what pattern to watch for next time.

## Questions

1. What originally made the idea look attractive?
2. What evidence contradicted the first impression?
3. Was the failure about demand, monetization, retention, build complexity, or distribution?
4. Did we confuse loud social chatter with actual willingness to pay?
5. Did we underestimate platform policy, competitor quality, or acquisition difficulty?
6. What reusable lesson should influence future idea screening?

## Output Format

```markdown
RESEARCH POSTMORTEM
- Idea:
- Why it looked promising:
- What killed it:
- Evidence that mattered most:
- Mistake in our prior thinking:
- Better heuristic next time:
- Promote to: memory | learnings | checklist | none
```

## Anti-Self-Deception Rule

Do not write comforting postmortems. Be plain.

Bad:
- "Interesting space, maybe revisit later" when the real issue is no one pays.

Good:
- "High chatter, low buyer intent; people complain but do not pay for solutions in this category."
